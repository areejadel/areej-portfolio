AraEduAgent — Arabic LLM Benchmark for Student Simulation IEEE Access · First author

Peer-reviewed · IEEE Access, Vol. 14 · Open access

The first Arabic adaptation of the EduAgent benchmark for generative student simulation, plus the full evaluation pipeline behind it. Benchmarks five LLMs — GPT-3.5 Turbo, GPT-4o Mini, Llama 3.1, Qwen2.5 and ALLaM-7B — on behavioural, cognitive and assessment metrics, validated against real eye-tracking (AOI) gaze data and quiz responses from university students. Designs and compares three context-reduction strategies, including LLMLingua-2 prompt compression with XLM-RoBERTa sanitisation.

HyMACS / DATR — Cross-Family Evaluation of LLM-Generated Lectures In preparation

Research project · in preparation for journal submission

A multi-agent pipeline that generates university-level lectures, grounds them in reference textbooks using retrieval-augmented generation (FAISS + MiniLM), and refines them with Dimension-Aware Targeted Refinement and a regression guard. Evaluation pairs an inner judge (GPT-4o) with a cross-family panel of external judges across six generators.

Key finding: models cannot meaningfully grade their own output — which motivated the cross-family panel. Reliability measured with Krippendorff's α, a self-preference matrix, and paired Wilcoxon significance testing.
